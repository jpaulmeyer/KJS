Position 1

1. Quantify! Tie real numbers into your accomplishments to take them to the next level. This lets employers know specifics about your past and what you can bring to the table.
2. Action words matter. Make sure to include verbs that best convey what you did at a past job. This shows you are active and passionate by telling employers concrete action you have taken.

3. Change is good. Talk about how each of your accomplishments and actions positively affected the company you worked for. This will get employers thinking about how you would benefit THEIR company.
4. No relevant experience? No problem. Focus your resume on your relevant and transferrable skills along with any related projects you may have done. Skills and experience can be gained in many ways, so show employers how you can be the best fit for them.
5. Sell yourself! You do not need to include everything, but highlight your best accomplishments and show future employers in your chosen field that you are ready for the job.
